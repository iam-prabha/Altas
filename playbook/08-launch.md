# 8. Launch

## The Principle

Launching isn't a single day. It's a process that starts weeks before you ship and continues for months after. The launch day itself is just the peak of a wave you've been building.

Your goal is not to go viral. It's to get your product in front of the right 100 people who will become your first paying customers. A targeted, personal launch to 100 relevant people beats a "viral" launch to 10,000 tire-kickers every time.

## The Checklist

### Pre-Launch (2-4 Weeks Before)

- [ ] Build a launch list: 50-200 people who might be interested. Include everyone who signed up during validation.
- [ ] Send a "coming soon" email to your list. No pitch. Just "I'm building X because Y. Launching on Z date."
- [ ] Line up 5-10 beta testers who will use the product before launch and give testimonials.
- [ ] Prepare launch assets: screenshots, a 30-second demo video, a one-liner, founder story.
- [ ] Write 5-10 different versions of your product description (for Reddit, HN, Twitter, email, etc.).
- [ ] Ensure your landing page, pricing page, and checkout work flawlessly. Test on mobile.
- [ ] Prepare a "launch week" content calendar: Twitter threads, LinkedIn posts, community comments.

### Launch Day Playbook

**Product Hunt:**
- [ ] Schedule launch for early morning (PT) — PH audience peaks in US morning.
- [ ] Your first-hour upvotes determine ranking. Rally your list to upvote immediately.
- [ ] Engage with every comment. Answer questions. Be helpful, not salesy.
- [ ] Don't make your launch just a link drop. Share the story behind the product.
- [ ] Results: PH launch can bring 1,000-5,000 visitors. Convert at 1-5%.

**Hacker News (Show HN):**
- [ ] Title must describe what you built in a neutral, factual way. "Show HN: I built a tool that X for Y."
- [ ] First comment should tell the story: why you built it, tech stack, what's next.
- [ ] Be ready to answer technical questions. HN audience is technical and skeptical.
- [ ] Don't astroturf (fake upvotes/accounts). HN detects and penalizes this.
- [ ] Results: HN front page = 5,000-50,000 visitors. Very spikey traffic.

**Niche Communities:**
- [ ] Reddit: Post in the most specific subreddit for your target audience. Not r/SaaS, r/yourniche.
- [ ] Indie Hackers: Write a "How I built X in Y weeks" post.
- [ ] Facebook / LinkedIn groups: Share as a "Here's what I'm working on, feedback welcome?"
- [ ] Slack / Discord communities: Share in #show-and-tell or #tools channels.
- [ ] Results: Lower volume (50-500 visits) but much higher conversion (5-15%).

**Email Launch:**
- [ ] Send to your launch list with a clear CTA: "Try it free for 14 days" or "Get started."
- [ ] Personalize the first batch: "Hey [name], you mentioned [pain point], so I built X."
- [ ] Follow up with non-openers once, 3 days later.
- [ ] Results: 20-40% open rate, 5-15% click rate on a warm list.

### Post-Launch (First 30 Days)

- [ ] Personally onboard every single signup. Send a welcome email from your personal address.
- [ ] Offer a 15-minute onboarding call to every paying customer in the first week.
- [ ] Ask every customer: "What almost stopped you from signing up?" Record the answers.
- [ ] Fix the blockers they mention. Ship fixes within 24-48 hours.
- [ ] Collect testimonials and case studies from happy customers.
- [ ] Double down on the acquisition channel that worked best. Ignore the rest.
- [ ] Don't build new features for 30 days. Only fix onboarding friction and bugs.
- [ ] Track: signups → activations → paid conversions per channel.

### Content-Led Growth (Ongoing)

- [ ] Write 1-2 high-quality articles per month targeting search terms your customers use.
- [ ] Repurpose content: one article → Twitter thread → LinkedIn post → newsletter.
- [ ] Answer questions on Stack Overflow, Quora, Reddit where your product is relevant.
- [ ] Build in public: share revenue numbers, learnings, and mistakes on Twitter/Indie Hackers.
- [ ] Tools: Screenshot API for social cards, Headless Chrome for blog screenshots, Canva for thumbnails.

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)

- [ ] Make the signup frictionless (Google OAuth, no credit card).
- [ ] Design the product so that using it creates sharing (Calendly: every meeting is a demo).
- [ ] In-app sharing: "Invite your team" / "Share this report" — make it one click.
- [ ] Free tier that's useful enough to attract users, limited enough to convert them.
- [ ] Time-based trial with feature unlocks on conversion.

## Case Study: Superhuman (Invite-Only Launch)

Superhuman launched as invite-only. This wasn't just marketing theater — it served real purposes:
- Controlled supply during early buggy stages.
- Created exclusivity and curiosity.
- Each invite was a referral mechanism.
- Early users felt like insiders.

**The cold email outreach:** Rahul Vohra (founder) personally emailed every new user to schedule a 30-minute onboarding call. This was unsustainable at scale, but at launch, it built raving fans who churned at near-zero rates.

**Key takeaway:** The tactics that don't scale at launch (personal onboarding, founder-led sales, manual follow-ups) are exactly what you should do. They build the foundation for growth.

## Case Study: Notion (Product Hunt → Community)

Notion launched on Product Hunt multiple times. Each time, they improved the product based on feedback:
- First launch (2016): Interesting but rough.
- Second launch (2018): Templates, better UX. Went viral on PH.
- Third launch: Teams features. Crossed into mainstream.

**The real growth engine:** Notion's community. User-generated templates, a passionate subreddit, and "Notion setups" becoming a content genre on YouTube. Notion didn't build this — they enabled it.

**Key takeaway:** A great launch can start your flywheel. But sustained growth comes from enabling your users to spread the product.

## Red Flags

| Mistake | Why It's Dangerous |
|---------|-------------------|
| Launching to crickets | Not the end of the world. Means you didn't build an audience. Go back to communities and build relationships. |
| Astroturfing / faking engagement | Destroys trust permanently on platforms like HN and Reddit. |
| Launching unfinished product | First impressions matter. Wait until the core loop works smoothly. |
| Focusing on virality over value | Viral without retention is a party where everyone leaves. |
| Not following up | Most signups won't convert on day 1. Nurture them. |
| Only launching once | You can launch multiple times (new features, new markets, new pricing). |
