# Customer Interview Guide

## Before You Start

**Goal:** Understand how your target customer currently solves the problem — without pitching your idea.

**Format:** 15-30 minute call. Record with permission.

**DO:**
- Ask open-ended questions.
- Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule).
- Ask "why" repeatedly until you understand root cause.

**DON'T:**
- Pitch your product.
- Ask "Would you use X?" (they'll say yes to be nice).
- Defend your idea when they criticize it.

---

## Interview Script

### Introduction (2 min)

> "Thanks for your time. I'm researching how [people in role] handle [problem area]. I'm not selling anything — just trying to understand the challenges. The conversation is confidential. Mind if I record for notes?"

### Warm-up Questions (3 min)

1. Tell me about your role. What does a typical day look like?
2. What tools do you use most in your work?

### Problem Discovery (10 min)

3. Walk me through the last time you had to [solve this problem].
4. What was frustrating about it?
5. What did you do to work around those frustrations?
6. How much time did you spend on it?
7. How much did it cost (tools, people, mistakes)?

### Current Solution (5 min)

8. What are you using today to solve this?
9. What do you like about it? What do you hate?
10. If you could wave a magic wand, what would be different?
11. Have you looked for alternatives? What stopped you from switching?

### Willingness to Pay (3 min)

12. If a tool existed that solved [the specific pain], how much would you expect to pay per month?
13. Would you pay for it yourself, or would your company pay?
14. What would make this a "must-have" versus a "nice-to-have"?

### Wrap-up (2 min)

15. Is there anyone else you'd recommend I talk to?
16. Can I follow up if I have more questions?

## After the Interview

- [ ] Transcribe or summarize within 24 hours.
- [ ] Tag the problem themes (e.g., "slow reporting," "import issues," "manual data entry").
- [ ] Note the price range they mentioned.
- [ ] Add to a master spreadsheet with column: Name, Role, Company, Problem, Price, Quotes.

## Pattern Detection

After 10 interviews, review:
- What problem was mentioned by 5+ people? → Your core feature.
- What price range came up most? → Your pricing anchor.
- What workarounds did people invent? → Your competitive advantage.
- What did they hate about existing solutions? → Your marketing copy.
